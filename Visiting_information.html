Operation by situation

Facility operation

For the safety of visitors, the 120th floor sky terrace will not be open depending on the weather.

  • - When a weather forecast that exceeds the advisory has been issued or is scheduled (heavy rain advisory, strong wind advisory, yellow sand advisory, etc.)
  • - 5mm of rainfall per hour
  • - When the temperature difference between indoor and outdoor temperature is 18 degrees or more

For the safety of visitors, the operating methods of the sky deck, sky terrace, and photo zone may change without notice depending on the number of passengers.

Notes on use

Information on searching for belongings and prohibited items

In order to create a safe and pleasant viewing environment, SEOUL SKY prohibits the following items from being brought in.
When entering the observatory, visitors' belongings are checked through security screening equipment, so we ask for your cooperation.

Prohibited items

  1. 1 type. Items that may endanger or frighten other guests (weapons, weapons, chemical liquids, etc.)
  2. 2 types. Items that may pose a threat to buildings and facilities (such as ignitable items such as cigarettes and lighters, items that can cause explosion such as butane gas, etc.)
  3. 3 types. Items that interfere with the creation of a comfortable viewing environment for guests (food, glass bottles, pets, photography radio equipment, large bags, vehicles (prams, etc.), sports equipment, electronic cigarettes, etc.) items that can be given, etc.)
  • * Please refer to the link for detailed items.
  • * Items recovered from the security checkpoint can be picked up at the B1F information desk only on the same day. (Storage up to 1 month)
  • * Wheelchair access is possible. However, for safe viewing, only the individual and one accompanying person will be guided through a separate route, so please cooperate with the staff.
  • * The search equipment uses electromagnetic waves that are harmless to the human body.

Guide to viewing etiquette and precautions

Rules of conduct for safe viewing

Visitors are requested to observe the following for a safe viewing environment in the observatory space.

  • Contents
  • 1. Smoking is prohibited in all areas of the building.
  • 2. Bringing in or eating outside food is prohibited.
  • 3. Please refrain from running or shouting in all areas of the observatory.
  • 4. When using the escalator, do not walk and hold the rail handles on both sides.
  • 5. On the sky deck or sky terrace, please refrain from drastic actions that may cause crowds or surprise other guests.
  • 6. Be careful not to throw objects out of the building or drop them downstairs.
  • 7. Please refrain from occupying one seat for a long time, taking a seat, using a tripod (selfie stick), etc., or taking up an excessive range of action, such as lying on the floor.
  • 8. If you need emergency quasi-drugs, please use the infirmary in the World Tower. (For medicines, a doctor's diagnosis is required)
  • 9. All guests, including pregnant women and the elderly, are requested to receive sufficient training from the staff in advance when using the Sky Deck or Sky Terrace.
  • 10. Filming and recording for commercial purposes in the observatory is, in principle, prohibited. (Cooperation with PR team if necessary)

Other notes

  1. 1. Behavior that harms other spectators or non-compliance with viewing etiquette may result in denial of entry or request to exit.
  2. 2. The company is not responsible for accidents, injuries, loss, theft, etc. caused by the negligence of the visitors.
  3. 3. In case of intentional damage to the facility while using the observatory, you will be held responsible for the repair.
  4. 4. During business hours, viewing may be restricted in some sections due to broadcast shooting and events.
  5. 5. Operating days and business hours of internal facilities and contents may change without prior notice according to business policies.
